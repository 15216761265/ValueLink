When does company need property valuation

Real estate properties are usually booked as non-current assets to most enterprises except real estate developers which records most of real estate properties as inventories. Enterprises generally acknowledge the neediness of property valuation due to accounting requirement such as purchase price allocation and asset impairment. However, they may not aware the neediness of property valuation from listing regulation perspective. For example: Stock Exchange of Hong Kong has strict policy requires IPO applicants to conduct property valuations.
  • If IPO applicants engaged in property developments, letting, property investments, they are required to obtain property valuation reports and disclose valuation information for all property interests of their property activities. However, there is exemption that to each applicant' s property interest has a carrying amount below 1% of the applicant's total assets. This exemption has a cap which is the total carrying amount of property interests that exempt valuation must not exceed 10% of the applicant's total assets
  • λ If IPO applicants did not engage in property developments, a property valuation report for each property interest of its non-property activities which has a carrying amount of 15% or more of its total assets is required.
The listing regulation requires property valuation on acquisition or disposal of any property interest, or of a company whose assets consist solely or mainly of property, where any of the percentage ratios is 25% or above.
